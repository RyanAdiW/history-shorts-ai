He conquered most of the known world… and died in bed at just 32.

Alexander the Great built an empire stretching from Greece to Egypt and all the way to India.

In 323 BCE, while in Babylon, he attended a banquet and suddenly became ill.

For nearly two weeks, he suffered from a high fever.

Day by day, he grew weaker… until he could no longer speak.

So what killed him?

Most historians believe it was a natural illness—possibly typhoid fever, malaria, or another serious infection.

Poisoning is a famous theory, but it remains debated and is considered less likely.

After surviving countless battles, Alexander may have been defeated by something far more ordinary: disease.

Even the greatest conqueror in history could not conquer death.
