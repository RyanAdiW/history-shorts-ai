TITLE:
Why Did Alexander the Great Die at Just 32?

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
Alexander the Great built one of the largest empires in ancient history, stretching from Greece to Egypt and into India. In June 323 BCE, at the height of his power, he suddenly fell ill and died in Babylon at only 32 years old.

KEY TIMELINE:

* 336 BCE — Alexander becomes king of Macedon after the assassination of his father, Philip II of Macedon.
* 334–326 BCE — Conquers the Persian Empire and campaigns as far as India.
* June 323 BCE — Becomes sick after a banquet in Babylon and dies after about 10–12 days of fever.

MAIN FIGURES:

* Alexander the Great: Macedonian king and military conqueror.
* Ptolemy I Soter: One of Alexander’s generals and later ruler of Egypt.
* Arrian: Major source for Alexander’s life.

KEY FACTS:

* Ancient accounts agree Alexander developed a high fever and gradually became too weak to speak.
* The most accepted modern explanation is a natural illness, such as malaria, typhoid fever, or another infection.
* Poisoning is a famous theory, but most historians consider it debated and less likely because his decline lasted many days.
* Years of wounds, exhaustion, heavy drinking, and stress may have weakened his body.

DRAMATIC ANGLE:
Alexander survived countless battles, but after conquering most of the known world, he was brought down not by an enemy army, but by a mysterious illness.

POSSIBLE HOOKS:

1. He conquered an empire—then died in bed at just 32.
2. Was Alexander the Great poisoned, or killed by disease?
3. The world’s greatest conqueror may have been defeated by a fever.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
Even the most powerful man in the world could not escape the limits of the human body.
