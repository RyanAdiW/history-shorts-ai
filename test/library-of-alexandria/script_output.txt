How did the greatest library in the ancient world simply disappear?

Built in Alexandria, Egypt, the Library of Alexandria was meant to collect all human knowledge. It may have held hundreds of thousands of scrolls on science, math, philosophy, and literature.

But it likely wasn’t destroyed in one dramatic fire.

In 48 BCE, a fire during Julius Caesar’s campaign may have damaged part of it. Later, wars, political turmoil, and shrinking support slowly weakened the library.

By 391 CE, the Serapeum—one of its last surviving branches—was destroyed.

And that was likely the end.

The real tragedy? We don’t know exactly what was lost.

Works from some of the greatest minds in history may have vanished forever.

The Library of Alexandria reminds us that knowledge is powerful… but also fragile.
