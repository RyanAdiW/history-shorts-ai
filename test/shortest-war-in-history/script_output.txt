The shortest war in history lasted just 38 minutes.

In 1896, the Sultan of Zanzibar died suddenly. His nephew, Khalid bin Barghash, seized the palace and declared himself the new ruler.

There was one problem.

Britain controlled Zanzibar’s foreign affairs—and they did not approve.

The British ordered Khalid to step down by 9:00 AM on August 27.

He refused.

Right on schedule, five British warships opened fire on the palace.

In less than 40 minutes, the palace was in ruins.

Hundreds of Zanzibaris were killed or wounded. The British had only one lightly injured sailor.

Khalid escaped to the German consulate, and Britain installed a new sultan.

A war that decided a nation’s future was over before most people finished breakfast.
