TITLE:
The Anglo-Zanzibar War: The Shortest War in History

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
On August 27, 1896, the United Kingdom and the Sultanate of Zanzibar fought what is widely recognized as the shortest war in recorded history. The conflict lasted about 38 to 45 minutes, depending on the source. It began when a new sultan took power without British approval.

KEY TIMELINE:

* August 25, 1896 — Sultan Hamad bin Thuwaini dies suddenly.
* August 25, 1896 — Khalid bin Barghash seizes the palace and declares himself sultan.
* August 27, 1896, 9:00 AM — British warships open fire after Khalid refuses to step down.
* August 27, 1896, around 9:38 AM — The palace is destroyed and the war ends.

MAIN FIGURES:

* Khalid bin Barghash: Claimant to the throne who resisted British demands.
* Hamad bin Thuwaini: Pro-British sultan whose death triggered the crisis.
* Basil Cave: British representative who delivered the ultimatum.
* Hamud bin Mohammed: British-backed ruler installed after the conflict.

KEY FACTS:

* The British gave Khalid an ultimatum to leave the palace by 9:00 AM on August 27.
* When he refused, five British warships bombarded the palace.
* Khalid fled to the German consulate.
* Zanzibar suffered hundreds of casualties, while the British had only one lightly wounded sailor.
* Most historians cite the war as lasting 38 minutes, though some sources say up to 45 minutes.

DRAMATIC ANGLE:
An entire war—ultimatum, bombardment, and surrender—was over in less time than a TV episode. It showed how overwhelming imperial power could decide the fate of a nation in under an hour.

POSSIBLE HOOKS:

1. The shortest war in history lasted less than your lunch break.
2. One sultan refused to step down—and lost his kingdom in 38 minutes.
3. Britain won a war before most people even knew it had started.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
The Anglo-Zanzibar War proves that in the age of empire, power imbalances could turn a war into a matter of minutes.
